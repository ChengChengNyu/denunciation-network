§ 02who → whom · 誰參誰

Who Reports on Whom

Rows are the reporter's office; columns the office of the official named. The routine channel runs steeply downhill: governors and governors-general reporting on county magistrates. The secret channel, by contrast, moved laterally among the elite — it is not shown in this matrix because office classification covers only part of its cast. Cell shade = number of ties.

§ 04the reporters · 具題者

The Chief Accusers

The twenty-five most active names in each channel — all figures of the public record. In the routine tab, board ministers (張廷玉, 朱軾, 隆科多) mostly process personnel cases institutionally and are flagged as such. In the secret tab, the same provincial chiefs who dominate the paper's story — 李衛, 田文鏡, 鄂爾泰 — denounce and recommend in person.

§ 05novelty · 鋒芒

Novel Targets, Old Feuds

In both channels, most officials are named once and never again — attention scattered wide. But the channels differ at the tail: the routine channel rarely piles on, while the secret channel shows the paper's signature patterns — pile-on targets, and denunciation answered by counter-denunciation.

Networks of Denunciation — Two Channels · 3,373 reporter→reported-on ties extracted from 24,545 proofread entries of the Yongzheng 吏科題本, joined by 1,836 denunciation (彈劾) and recommendation (保舉) ties from the secret palace-memorial corpus (硃批奏摺).

Routine-channel extraction is surname-anchored (a real surname required; office, place, and verb words blocked) from OCR-corrected text; roughly 5–10% residual noise remains. Reign dates parse for 95% of routine ties; action types are approximate — ties are matched back to same-day entries by the same reporter (93.7% match) and tagged by subject-line keywords, so 「參劾」-type wording marks the disciplinary third. Secret-channel ties carry their own 彈劾/保舉 coding from the research dataset (69 undated, 20 dated 1736: in the network, not the tempo chart). Sender offices follow the project's officeholder classification (Academia Sinica reference), which covers 62% of secret senders — unmatched figures appear unclassified. Board-and-court senders in the routine channel largely reflect institutional processing rather than personal accusation.
What this page does not contain. No memorial text, no place names attached to anonymized individuals, no day-level dates, and no raw edge table are embedded. Only 70 officials are named — the 25 most active reporters in each channel, 13 independently verified senior figures, and the secret channel's already-contested "pile-on" targets (≥3 accusers); every other official appears as an anonymous rank (「知縣」, "a county magistrate"). Ties where both endpoints are named — and thus readable — number 118 of 5,209 (2.3%), essentially the famous cases of the standard histories. The underlying datasets are unpublished and part of ongoing joint research; this page is a visualization, not a data release.
